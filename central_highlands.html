Central Highlands

Sri Lanka's highland is situated in the South Central part of the island where the altitude is 2,500 m from the sea level. Highlands are consits of cool greeny ravines, tumbling waterfalls, tea estates, and specially with extraordinary range of fauna and flora including several endangered species. Central highland in Sri Lanka was declared as a World heritage site in 2010 as it was recognized as one of a richest biodiversity area in the world. Nuwara-Eliya is the capital of highlands and it is also known as 'Little England'. Pidurutalagala or Mt. Pedro (2,524 m / 8,281 ft) , the tallest mountain in Sri Lanka is also located closer to Nuwara Eliya.


The temperature of the area is vary from 27 degree Celcius in the day and to 5 degree Celcius in the evening with thick mist.
